Our Menu

Chicken is an incredibly versatile protein that appears in a myriad of dishes across the globe, each culture adding its own unique twist and creating a rich tapestry of flavors, textures, and cooking techniques. In India, Chicken Tikka Masala is a crowd favorite, featuring chunks of marinated chicken grilled to perfection and then simmered in a creamy tomato-based curry sauce. The marinade typically includes yogurt and a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric, which impart a deep flavor to the chicken. The rich sauce, made with tomatoes, cream, and butter, complements the smoky grilled chicken perfectly. It is often served with naan bread or rice, creating a harmonious balance of heat and creaminess in every bite. Moving over to China, General Tso’s Chicken stands out in Chinese-American cuisine with its sweet and slightly spicy sauce coating crispy, deep-fried chicken pieces. Named after a Qing Dynasty military leader, the dish’s origins are rooted more in American-Chinese restaurants than traditional Chinese cooking. The sauce typically includes soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and sugar, creating a flavorful glaze that is irresistible when paired with steamed broccoli and white rice. The contrast of the crunchy exterior and the succulent interior of the chicken pieces makes it a beloved dish among many. In the Philippines, Chicken Adobo reigns supreme as a national dish, showcasing a harmonious blend of tangy, savory, and slightly sweet flavors. The chicken is marinated in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns, then simmered until tender. The vinegar and soy sauce balance each other beautifully, while the garlic and bay leaves add depth and aroma. Often served with steamed rice, Chicken Adobo is a comforting dish that reflects the simplicity and richness of Filipino cuisine. In France, Coq au Vin is a classic that translates to “rooster in wine,” traditionally using rooster or chicken slow-cooked in red wine with mushrooms, onions, bacon, and garlic. The dish is rich and flavorful, with the wine creating a deep, hearty sauce that perfectly complements the tender chicken. Herbs like thyme, parsley, and bay leaves add aromatic complexity. Coq au Vin is typically served with crusty French bread or mashed potatoes, making it a comforting and indulgent meal that showcases the elegance of French cuisine. Italy offers Chicken Cacciatore, meaning “hunter’s chicken,” a rustic dish featuring chicken braised in a sauce of tomatoes, onions, bell peppers, mushrooms, and wine. Often seasoned with herbs like rosemary and thyme, this dish captures the essence of Italian home cooking with its hearty and robust flavors. Chicken Cacciatore is often served with pasta or crusty bread to soak up the flavorful sauce, making it a beloved dish in many households. Ukraine brings us Chicken Kiev, a beloved dish where a chicken breast is pounded thin, wrapped around a piece of herbed butter, breaded, and then fried or baked until golden and crispy. When cut open, the melted butter inside flows out, creating a deliciously rich and flavorful experience. Typically served with mashed potatoes or a fresh salad, Chicken Kiev offers a delightful contrast between the crispy exterior and the tender, buttery chicken. In Mozambique, Peri-Peri Chicken is a spicy and flavorful delight, influenced by both local traditions and Portuguese cuisine. The chicken is marinated in a sauce made from peri-peri (bird’s eye) chilies, garlic, lemon juice, and herbs, giving it a fiery kick and tangy flavor.
